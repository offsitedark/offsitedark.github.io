Using the Zola

∵ OffsiteDark ∴ 2024-11-28 ∞ 2'

I had wanted to make a devblog to add to my X profile, as one would expect of any profile that's tring to be more "signal" than "noise" on the platform. I had found the Zola framework from looking through diff profiles that I follow, and came across @cunjur 's blog char.blog

At the time, they were using a Zola theme - Serene - and I had made my own version. I worked on it between moments when I had time during my wagie shift, and got it working like I wanted, but I had no content - I also wanted to make the dark version a bit darker. I DM-ed @cunjur aka char to talk with them about it, and got some of the kinks fixed (profile img not loading, colors), but by the end of the week char had moved from Zola to something else lol - Obsidian Publish.

Another good option, but I'll stick with this for now.

I had also saw @vmfunc 's aka mel's devblog/websites and thought the vibes immaculate, also the webneko (cat running after the cursor) a must have, so I worked on implementing that.

I switched from Serene to Halve-Z - https://www.getzola.org/themes/halve-z/ - I couldn't get it to load the right the way for the life of me, so I pivoted again to this current old-looking ass ZOLA.386 theme https://github.com/lopes/zola.386

It looks cool, but is hard as fuck to read on. I don't know how comfortable it'd be to use it long term.

I got the neko cursor to work after adding it to the JS pages and including it on the index.html page to load on all sites. Tried 3 diff ones until I got one that wasn't slow as fuck and where I could easily change the speed and img/gif if I wanted to.

That's about it.
